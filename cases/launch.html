Situation

I joined a telehealth and pharmacy platform during a corporate acquisition and was handed ownership of a prior authorization application. The app had been built to replace a manual PA process and was scheduled to go live within weeks of my start date.

It launched. It flopped. The old manual process was faster, easier to follow a PA through to a provider, and easier to track follow-ups. The new system was visually polished but functionally unusable. Customer service reps, pharmacists, managers, and providers were all struggling. Providers were getting duplicate outreach because the system could not track who had already been contacted.

Diagnosis

The product had been built from a spec, not from an understanding of how people actually worked. Nobody had mapped the real workflows of the people using it.

The system looked right on screen but broke down in practice because it did not match how PAs actually move through an organization.

PAs move across multiple roles with different information needs at each stage. Follow-up cycles require clear visibility into what has been done and what has not. The system had none of that. It had been built from assumptions about the process, not from the process itself.

What I Did

01 Identified every user type in the PA process: customer service reps, pharmacists, managers, providers. Each had different information needs at different points in the workflow.
02 Mapped their current workflows using the old manual system. Not what they were supposed to do. What they actually did.
03 Conducted time studies to understand where the real bottlenecks were in both the old and new systems.
04 Walked through the new system step by step against those workflows to identify exactly where it broke down for each role.
05 Fixed the critical bottlenecks first to match the speed of the legacy system. That restored trust before any additional improvements were made.
06 Planned and delivered iterative improvements that made the system better than what they had before, not just equivalent to it.
